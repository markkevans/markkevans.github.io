Guide for Recruiters

Apr 19th, 2014 | Comments

This is for all of the recruiters who call me at work, add me on Linkedin or send me speculative CV’s / roles for my consideration. Lets call it a best practice guide for how I want to interact with you, follow this guide and I am sure we will get on well, ignore this guide and I will be sure to not deal with you at all no matter how good the candidate or the role might be.

The aim of this guide is to help both myself and the recruiters who wish to get in contact with me, after all we are all busy people right?

LinkedIn

Do not add me on LinkedIn as ‘We have done business together’ if we have not, I will reject your request and I will file a spam complaint.

If you want to work with me, send me details of why I should work with you and include some references that I can contact for feedback in the intiial message, if you don’t hear back from me take that as a sign that I don’t need your services at this time, try again in 3 months.

Speculative CV’s

Do not send me speculative CV’s for a candidate who “You don’t want to miss out on”. If I am not working with you then you are unlikely to know what I am recruiting for or if I am recruiting at all.

Any speculative CV’s sent over will be considered a gift and will be treated as such no matter what terms you specify in your email, I do not accept your terms unless I explicity agree in writing.

Constant Calling / Emailing

Don’t call me constanntly without leaving a message on my voicemail, if you want to talk with me then call me one, leave me a voicemail message on why I should call you back. If you don’t receive a phonecall or email from me then please take that as a sign that I don’t need your services.

If you send me an email, explain why I should work with you and how you operate and wait for me to reply. If no reply is received then consider that confirmation that I don’t need your services at this time, try again in 3 months.

Calling my team

Do not call anyone who works for me to try and get them to put you through to me and do not call them offering an exciting new role, I do not work with recruiters / agencies who work in this way.

Meetups

I view all meetups organised by recruitment agencies as a business event rather than a meetup, please ensure you make it clear what benefit I will get from attending or from sending someone from my team.
